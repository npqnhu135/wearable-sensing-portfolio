# Track 3 — ECG Front-End: Documented Failure Mode

An AD8232 single-lead ECG analog front-end was assembled and recorded for
9.3 minutes. **The acquired signal is not usable for QRS/HR analysis.** This
folder documents the failure mode and proposes a specific hardware fix.

This negative result is included intentionally — reproducibility in low-resource
hardware projects requires documenting failure modes, not just successful traces.

## What was attempted

- **Front-end:** AD8232 single-lead ECG analog front-end
- **ADC:** ESP32-S3, 250 Hz sampling (verified jitter < 1 sample)
- **Electrodes:** 3× Ag/AgCl gel pads in Lead-I configuration
- **Recording duration:** 559 seconds (139,766 samples)

## What was observed

**70.1 % of samples sat at one of the two ADC rails (0 or 4095).** Spectral
inspection of the remaining 30 % revealed a ~30 Hz rail-to-rail oscillation
consistent with the AD8232 output stage running its internal DC-restoration
loop against an over-driven input rather than producing a biological signal.
No QRS complexes, P-waves, or T-waves were recoverable.

![ECG diagnostic](figures/fig_ecg_diagnostic.png)

## Root-cause hypothesis

The most parsimonious explanation:

1. The AD8232 **REF pin was tied to GND** rather than a Vcc/2 voltage divider
2. Under single-supply 3.3 V operation, skin-electrode DC offsets exceed the
   amplifier's input common-mode range
3. The right-leg drive (RLD) circuit alone cannot recover from this
   configuration; the output saturates at one rail and the internal DC servo
   bounces it back, causing the observed periodic oscillation

## Proposed fix

The next hardware revision will:

1. Add a Vcc/2 voltage divider with 1 µF decoupling on the REF pin
2. Verify SDN is pulled high externally rather than left floating
3. Move from USB-powered (laptop, ground loops) to 9 V battery operation with
   LM7805-regulated 3.3 V isolated supply for AD8232

## How to reproduce the diagnostic

```bash
pip install pandas numpy matplotlib
jupyter lab analysis.ipynb
```

Run all cells. The figure `figures/fig_ecg_diagnostic.png` is regenerated from
`data/ecg_raw_250hz.csv`.

## Why include a failure mode in a portfolio

A clean negative result with root-cause analysis and a specific remediation plan
is higher information density than a re-recorded "working" trace whose
provenance is undocumented. PIs evaluating undergraduate research portfolios
generally prefer scientific honesty over polished outcomes — particularly when
the failure analysis demonstrates understanding of the underlying circuit.
